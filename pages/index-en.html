Rich History

Najran's roots date back to the second millennium BC, and it was an important part of the frankincense and myrrh trade routes. Najran houses the archaeological site of Al-Ukhdood, which is considered one of the important archaeological sites in the region, containing remains of an ancient settlement, inscriptions, and artifacts dating back thousands of years.

Diverse Geography

Najran is located in the southwest of the Kingdom, near the Yemeni border, and encompasses Wadi Najran and scenic plains and plateaus, in addition to fertile agricultural oases. It features geographical diversity including mountains, valleys, and deserts, providing stunning natural landscapes.

Promising Economy

Development activities and investments are expanding in Najran, especially in the mining and mineral resources sector. The region includes promising mineral resources with exploration operations for copper, gold, and other minerals. It is also famous for agriculture and oases that produce the finest types of dates.

Historical Timeline

2000 BC

Ancient Times

Najran's roots date back to the second millennium BC, and it was an important part of the frankincense and myrrh trade routes, and was traversed by civilizations of southern Arabia. The archaeological city of Al-Ukhdood was established, which is considered one of the important archaeological sites in the region.

1st Century

Christian Era

Najran witnessed a clear Christian presence before Islam, with churches and communities, and was mentioned in early historical sources. Najran was an important religious and cultural center in the region.

7th Century

Entry of Islam

Najran joined the Islamic state in early times, and went through successive historical stages. It became part of the Islamic state and flourished as a commercial and cultural center.

1934

Joining the Kingdom

Najran joined the modern Kingdom of Saudi Arabia in 1934. Since then, the city has witnessed continuous development in all fields while preserving its authentic historical and cultural heritage.
